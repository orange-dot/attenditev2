Poštovani,

Obraćam Vam se kao ARGUS udruženja građana "Lišeni Svega" sa predlogom koji verujemo da može doprineti unapređenju sistema socijalne zaštite u Republici Srbiji.

### Kontekst našeg angažmana

Od novembra 2025. godine sprovodimo analizu postupanja institucija socijalne zaštite, a od 2023. godine pratimo i zdravstveni sistem, koristeći mehanizme Zakona o pristupu informacijama od javnog značaja. Kroz ovaj proces prikupili smo značajan broj odgovora koji ukazuju na prostor za unapređenje međuagencijske koordinacije - tema koja je, koliko smo upoznati, prepoznata i u strateškim dokumentima Vlade.

Naši nalazi dostupni su na: https://lisenisvega.rs

### Ključna zapažanja iz prakse

Tokom dva meseca sistematske analize, uključujući FOI zahteve upućene svim centrima za socijalni rad u Srbiji (33), dokumentovali smo sledeće sistemske obrasce:

#### A. Institucionalna fragmentacija
- Zdravstvene ustanove u istom gradu nemaju pristup podacima jedne o drugima
- Građani moraju fizički nositi dokumentaciju između institucija
- Sistemi za elektronsku razmenu podataka postoje ali se masovno ne koriste
- Domovi zdravlja nemaju podatke iz bolnica, CSR nema podatke iz zdravstvenog sistema, gerontološki centri nemaju podatke iz CSR-a

#### B. Odsustvo sistemskog praćenja kvaliteta
- Većina centara za socijalni rad ne vodi evidenciju odbijenih zahteva za usluge
- Ne postoji praćenje žalbi korisnika i njihovih ishoda
- Nemoguće je utvrditi kvalitet odlučivanja prvostepenih organa jer se negativne odluke sistematski ne dokumentuju
- Neki centri zahtevaju 40 dana da utvrde da li poseduju sopstvene pravilnike

#### C. Neregulisani sukobi interesa
- Većina CSR-ova nema protokol za situacije kada je centar istovremeno staratelj i procenitelj potreba istog lica
- Ovo stvara sistemski rizik za nepravilnosti u postupanju

#### D. Prebacivanje javnih funkcija na građane
- Obaveze javnih službi se sistematski prenose na srodnike
- Gotovo sve službe prvo definišu šta NIJE njihov posao (uočen obrazac u većini interakcija)
- Građani obavljaju birokratski posao umesto da se institucije bave svojom funkcijom
- Ovaj obrazac je toliko normalizovan da se više ne prepoznaje kao problem

**Studija slučaja: Kružno prebacivanje odgovornosti**

Lično iskustvo sa slepim invalidom koji je imao dokumentovane padove na glavu opasne po život:

1. **CSR**: "To nije naš posao, obratite se policiji"
2. **Policija**: "Mi ne možemo ništa, to je posao CSR-a"
3. **Zdravstvene ustanove**: "Nije naša nadležnost, kontaktirajte CSR"
4. **CSR ponovo**: "Ne možemo ništa bez naloga policije"

Za to vreme, slepi invalid je ostavljen da se kocka sa životom kod kuće - uprkos tome što su **sve institucije bile upoznate** sa dokumentovanim opasnim padovima.

Originalni "plan" svih organa bio je: **"Ne može drugačije"** - uz prebacivanje odgovornosti u krug i čekanje da praznici prođu.

**Ishod**: Tek nakon upornog insistiranja i prisiljavanja institucija na međusobnu komunikaciju, slepi invalid je konačno smešten u bolnicu pre praznika. Ono što je navodno bilo "nemoguće" - pokazalo se sasvim mogućim kada je građanin odbio da prihvati sistemsku inerciju.

Ovaj slučaj ilustruje zašto je platforma za koordinaciju neophodna: **nijedna institucija nije imala uvid u postupanje ostalih**, svaka je delovala izolovano, i samo je uporan pritisak pojedinca sprečio potencijalno tragičan ishod

#### E. Izgaranje zaposlenih usled besmislene birokratije

Lično sam prisustvovao sceni u Opštoj bolnici Kikinda gde je zdravstveni radnik na prijemu vikao na građanina koji je došao da se raspita za poznаnika hitno primljenog u bolnicu. Razlog frustracije radnika: "Vidiš da radim nešto drugo" - pri čemu je "drugo" bila papirologija koja ne generiše korisne informacije za bilo koga.

**Ovo nije kritika zaposlenih** - to je sistemski problem. Zdravstveni radnici troše vreme na procedure koje ne služe pacijentima, a rezultat je:
- Izgaranje (burnout) zaposlenih
- Smanjen kvalitet interakcije sa korisnicima
- Frustracija na obe strane

**Cilj platforme nije kontrola radnika** - već eliminacija besmislenih procedura i automatizacija rutinskih zadataka, kako bi se zaposleni mogli baviti onim zbog čega su izabrali svoje profesije: brigom o ljudima.

Srbija već ima funkcionalan primer: **[eZUP sistem](https://www.ite.gov.rs/tekst/en/12/euprava-portal.php)** (2.5 miliona registrovanih korisnika) uspešno eliminiše putovanja građana po uverenja. Predložena platforma bi proširila ovaj pristup na koordinaciju između različitih sektora.

#### F. Pozitivni primeri
- 72% centara za socijalni rad je odgovorilo na FOI zahtev u roku od 48 sati
- Pojedine institucije (sudovi, pojedini CSR-ovi) pokazuju da sistem MOŽE funkcionisati profesionalno i transparentno
- Ovi primeri demonstriraju da je promena moguća uz sistemski pristup

#### G. Studija slučaja: Međuinstitucionalni vakuum

**Slučaj iz prakse zdravstvene inspekcije:** Pacijentkinja (60+ godina) sa karcinomom debelog creva lečena u dve ustanove - regionalnoj bolnici i referentnom onkološkom centru.

**Utvrđeni sistemski propusti:**

1. **Nepostojanje razmene podataka:** U informacionom sistemu regionalne bolnice "nema evidencije" da se pacijentkinja javila za zakazivanje terapije - dok referentni centar ima drugačije podatke. Nijedna ustanova nije imala uvid u kompletnu sliku.

2. **Samopotvrđujući interni nadzor:** Obe ustanove su sprovele vanrednu unutrašnju proveru kvaliteta stručnog rada. Obe komisije su zaključile da su "sve mere bile u skladu sa medicinskom doktrinom" - uprkos očiglednom propustu u koordinaciji.

3. **Strukturni kapacitetni deficit:** Izveštaj referentnog centra eksplicitno navodi da je za područje sa ~2 miliona stanovnika dostupan samo JEDAN referentni centar za određene vrste terapije - što stvara sistemski bottleneck.

**Zašto je platforma neophodna:** Da je postojao sistem koji omogućava uvid u postupanje svih ustanova, propust bi bio detektovan u realnom vremenu - pre nego što je pacijentkinja "ispala" između institucija.

#### H. Studija slučaja: Administrativni vakuum

**Slučaj iz prakse:** Pacijentkinja (29 godina) sa metastatskim karcinomom. Nadležni lekar je uputio zahtev fondu zdravstvenog osiguranja za odobrenje nastavka lečenja.

**Hronologija propusta:**
- Zahtev primljen i inicijalno obrađen (zatraženi dodatni podaci)
- Podaci dostavljeni istog dana
- **112 dana bez odgovora**
- Pacijentkinja umrla

**Sistemska implikacija:** Lekar nije mogao da nastavi lečenje (nema odobrenje) niti da obavesti pacijentkinju o konačnoj odluci (odluka nije doneta). Pacijentkinja je ostala u administrativnom limbu.

**Kapacitet platforme:** AI sistem može detektovati zahteve koji predugo čekaju na odgovor i automatski eskalirati slučajeve. Nijedan zahtev onkološkog pacijenta ne bi smeo da ostane bez odgovora 112 dana.

### Predlog za razmatranje

Na osnovu analize, pripremili smo tehničku specifikaciju za platformu koja bi mogla olakšati koordinaciju između javnih službi. Specifikacija se oslanja na iskustva zemalja koje su uspešno implementirale slične sisteme:

- Estonija ([X-Road platforma](https://e-estonia.com/solutions/interoperability-services/x-road/), 929+ povezanih institucija)
- Finska i Danska (regionalna saradnja kroz [NIIS](https://www.niis.org/))
- Ukrajina ([Diia platforma](https://ukraine.ua/diia-the-catalyst-of-ukraines-e-governance-revolution/), 23 miliona korisnika)

Predložena arhitektura uzima u obzir zahteve Evropskog okvira za interoperabilnost, što može biti od značaja u kontekstu procesa evropskih integracija.

### Napomena o tehnologiji

Za pripremu ove analize, dokumentacije i implementaciju MVP verzije platforme (koju dostavljamo uz ovo pismo) korišćen je Anthropic AI model **Claude Opus 4.5** (claude-opus-4-5-20251101) - trenutno najnapredniji Anthropic model. Međutim, za potrebe vladinog sistema postoje open-source modeli dobrih performansi koji mogu biti hostovani lokalno. Celokupan sistem, uključujući korisničko sučelje, može se implementirati u vladinim kapacitetima - na primer u [Državnom data centru u Kragujevcu](https://www.ite.gov.rs/tekst/en/34/government-data-centre-in-kragujevac.php) (EN 50600 Class 4 sertifikat, prvi takav u Jugoistočnoj Evropi) - čime se obezbeđuje puna kontrola nad podacima i infrastrukturom.

Za medicinsku AI komponentu predlažemo kombinaciju open-source modela:
- **[OpenBioLLM-70B](https://huggingface.co/aaditya/Llama3-OpenBioLLM-70B)** (Llama-3 baziran) - nadmašuje GPT-4 i Med-PaLM na medicinskim benchmarkovima
- **[DeepSeek-R1](https://github.com/deepseek-ai/DeepSeek-R1)** (MIT licenca) - opšti reasoning sa chain-of-thought objašnjenjima
- **[GLM-4.5V](https://github.com/THUDM/GLM-4)** - vision-language model za analizu medicinskih slika

Celokupna dokumentacija i kod su objavljeni pod **AGPL v3 licencom** - čime se osigurava da javni resursi ostanu javni i da se sprečava privatizacija javno finansiranog koda.

### Bezbednost open source pristupa

Open source pristup vladinim sistemima nije bezbednosni rizik - **to je bezbednosna prednost**. Ovaj princip je poznat u kriptografiji već 140+ godina kao [Kerkhoffsov princip (1883)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kerckhoffs's_principle): sistem mora biti siguran čak i ako je njegova arhitektura javna. Svi moderni bezbednosni standardi (HTTPS, AES, RSA) su javno objavljeni - i upravo zato su sigurni, jer ih pregledaju hiljade nezavisnih stručnjaka.

Detaljno tehničko obrazloženje ovog pristupa, uključujući izbor Go programskog jezika i modularnog monolita arhitekture, dostupno je u prilogu: **`docs/technical-rationale.md`**

### Automatska detekcija opasnih grešaka

Jedan od ključnih kapaciteta predložene platforme je **AI-bazirana detekcija logičkih nekonzistentnosti** u medicinskoj i socijalnoj dokumentaciji. Sistem bi automatski identifikovao situacije poput:

- **Nemoguća medicinska uputstva** - npr. uputstvo pacijentu sa dijagnostikovanom slepoćom (retinopatija oba oka) da "čita vrednosti i zapisuje"
- **Nekonzistentnost između dijagnoze i preporuke** - npr. zaključak da je postupanje "u skladu sa dobrom praksom" kada je pacijent otpušten sa glikemijom od 0.7 mmol/L i izjavom da "nega nije obezbeđena"

Ovakvi slučajevi su dokumentovani tokom naše analize i predstavljaju sistemski rizik za javno zdravlje. AI sistem može da detektuje ove nekonzistentnosti u realnom vremenu, pre nego što dovedu do štetnih posledica.

### Dokazana efikasnost kombinacije lekar + AI

Istraživanja pokazuju da kombinacija lekara i AI sistema značajno prevazilazi performanse svakog pojedinačno:

- **66% lekara u SAD koristi AI u 2024.** - porast od 78% u odnosu na 2023. ([American Medical Association](https://www.ama-assn.org/practice-management/digital-health/2-3-physicians-are-using-health-ai-78-2023))
- **AI u radiologiji postiže 94% senzitivnosti** u detekciji plućnih nodula, naspram 65-88% kod radiologa ([Nature Communications Medicine](https://www.nature.com/articles/s43856-023-00388-5))
- **90% senzitivnosti u detekciji karcinoma dojke** naspram 78% kod radiologa ([Lancet Digital Health](https://www.thelancet.com/journals/landig/article/PIIS2589-7500(19)30235-2/fulltext))
- **FDA je odobrila ~950 AI medicinskih uređaja** do avgusta 2024. ([NCBI](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK613808/))
- **AI smanjuje vreme dokumentacije do 70%** - lekar se fokusira na pacijenta, ne na papire ([Mayo Clinic Proceedings: Digital Health](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11975979/))

Ključ je **kolaboracija**: AI ima pristup kompletnoj medicinskoj istoriji i može identifikovati obrasce koje čovek ne može, dok lekar donosi konačne odluke koristeći kliničko iskustvo i kontekst. Ni AI sam, ni lekar sam - zajedno su najefikasniji.

## English version (Human Rights Watch)

**To:** Human Rights Watch - Netherlands Office
**Subject:** Civil society initiative for improved public service coordination in Serbia

---

Dear,

I hope this message finds you well. I am writing as ARGUS of the Serbian citizens' association "Lišeni Svega," to share an update on our ongoing work and a constructive proposal we have developed.

### Background

Since November 2025, we have been conducting systematic analysis of social protection institutions in Serbia, and since 2023 we have been monitoring the healthcare system, through freedom of information mechanisms. This process has yielded insights suggesting opportunities for improved inter-agency coordination - challenges that, we understand, are recognized in various government strategic documents as well.

Our documented findings are available at: https://lisenisvega.rs

### Key observations from practice

Over two months of systematic analysis, including FOI requests sent to all social work centers in Serbia (33), we have documented the following systemic patterns:

#### A. Institutional fragmentation
- Healthcare facilities in the same city lack access to each other's data
- Citizens must physically carry documentation between institutions
- Electronic data exchange systems exist but are massively underutilized
- Primary care centers lack hospital data, social work centers lack healthcare data, gerontology centers lack social work center data

#### B. Absence of systematic quality monitoring
- Most social work centers do not maintain records of rejected service requests
- There is no tracking of user complaints and their outcomes
- It is impossible to assess the quality of first-instance decision-making as negative decisions are systematically not documented
- Some centers require 40 days to determine whether they possess their own internal regulations

#### C. Unregulated conflicts of interest
- Most social work centers lack protocols for situations where the center is simultaneously guardian and needs assessor for the same person
- This creates systemic risk for procedural irregularities

#### D. Transfer of public functions to citizens
- Obligations of public services are systematically transferred to relatives
- Nearly all services first define what is NOT their responsibility (pattern observed in most interactions)
- Citizens perform bureaucratic work instead of institutions fulfilling their function
- This pattern has become so normalized that it is no longer recognized as a problem

**Case study: Circular deflection of responsibility**

Personal experience with a blind disabled person who had documented life-threatening falls:

1. **Social Work Center (CSR)**: "This is not our job, contact the police"
2. **Police**: "We can't do anything, this is CSR's job"
3. **Healthcare facilities**: "Not our jurisdiction, contact CSR"
4. **CSR again**: "We can't act without a police order"

Meanwhile, the blind disabled person was left to gamble with their life at home - despite **all institutions being aware** of the documented dangerous falls.

The original "plan" of all agencies was: **"There's no other way"** - while deflecting responsibility in circles and waiting for the holidays to pass.

**Outcome**: Only after persistent insistence and forcing institutions to communicate with each other was the blind disabled person finally admitted to the hospital before the holidays. What was supposedly "impossible" - turned out to be entirely possible when a citizen refused to accept systemic inertia.

This case illustrates why a coordination platform is essential: **no institution had visibility into the actions of others**, each operated in isolation, and only persistent pressure from an individual prevented a potentially tragic outcome

#### E. Worker burnout due to meaningless bureaucracy

I personally witnessed a scene at Kikinda General Hospital where a healthcare worker at the reception desk was shouting at a citizen who came to inquire about an acquaintance who had been urgently admitted. The reason for the worker's frustration: "Can't you see I'm doing something else" - where "something else" was paperwork that generates no useful information for anyone.

**This is not a criticism of the workers** - it is a systemic problem. Healthcare workers spend time on procedures that don't serve patients, and the result is:
- Employee burnout
- Reduced quality of interaction with users
- Frustration on both sides

**The goal of the platform is not to control workers** - but to eliminate meaningless procedures and automate routine tasks, so that employees can focus on what they chose their professions for: caring for people.

Serbia already has a functional example: **[eZUP system](https://www.ite.gov.rs/tekst/en/12/euprava-portal.php)** (2.5 million registered users) successfully eliminates citizens' trips to collect certificates. The proposed platform would extend this approach to coordination between different sectors.

#### F. Positive examples
- 72% of social work centers responded to FOI requests within 48 hours
- Certain institutions (courts, some social work centers) demonstrate that the system CAN function professionally and transparently
- These examples demonstrate that change is possible with a systematic approach

#### G. Case study: Inter-institutional vacuum

**Case from health inspection practice:** A patient (60+ years old) with colon cancer treated at two institutions - a regional hospital and a reference oncology center.

**Identified systemic failures:**

1. **Absence of data exchange:** The regional hospital's information system showed "no evidence" that the patient came to schedule therapy - while the reference center had different records. Neither institution had visibility into the complete picture.

2. **Self-validating internal oversight:** Both institutions conducted extraordinary internal quality reviews. Both commissions concluded that "all measures were in accordance with medical doctrine" - despite the obvious coordination failure.

3. **Structural capacity deficit:** The reference center's report explicitly states that for an area with ~2 million inhabitants, only ONE reference center is available for certain types of therapy - creating a systemic bottleneck.

**Why the platform is essential:** If a system existed that enabled visibility into all institutions' actions, the failure would have been detected in real-time - before the patient "fell through the cracks" between institutions.

#### H. Case study: Administrative vacuum

**Case from practice:** A patient (29 years old) with metastatic cancer. The attending physician submitted a request to the health insurance fund for approval to continue treatment.

**Timeline of failure:**
- Request received and initially processed (additional data requested)
- Data provided the same day
- **112 days without response**
- Patient died

**Systemic implication:** The physician could not continue treatment (no approval) nor inform the patient of the final decision (no decision was made). The patient remained in administrative limbo.

**Platform capability:** An AI system can detect requests that have been waiting too long for a response and automatically escalate cases. No oncology patient's request should remain unanswered for 112 days.

### A constructive proposal

Building on our analysis, we have prepared technical specifications for an interoperability platform drawing on successful implementations in Estonia ([X-Road](https://e-estonia.com/solutions/interoperability-services/x-road/), connecting 929+ institutions), Finland, Denmark, and Ukraine ([Diia](https://ukraine.ua/diia-the-catalyst-of-ukraines-e-governance-revolution/), serving 23 million citizens).

The proposed architecture is designed with European Interoperability Framework requirements in mind, which may be particularly relevant given Serbia's EU accession process.

### A note on technology

This analysis, documentation, and the MVP platform implementation (which we are submitting alongside this letter) were prepared using Anthropic's **Claude Opus 4.5** (claude-opus-4-5-20251101) - currently Anthropic's most advanced model. However, for government system purposes, there are well-performing open-source models that can be hosted locally. The entire system, including the user interface, can be deployed within government infrastructure - for example, at the [State Data Center in Kragujevac](https://www.ite.gov.rs/tekst/en/34/government-data-centre-in-kragujevac.php) (EN 50600 Class 4 certified, first such in Southeast Europe) - ensuring full control over data and infrastructure.

For the medical AI component, we propose a combination of open-source models:
- **[OpenBioLLM-70B](https://huggingface.co/aaditya/Llama3-OpenBioLLM-70B)** (Llama-3 based) - outperforms GPT-4 and Med-PaLM on medical benchmarks
- **[DeepSeek-R1](https://github.com/deepseek-ai/DeepSeek-R1)** (MIT license) - general reasoning with chain-of-thought explanations
- **[GLM-4.5V](https://github.com/THUDM/GLM-4)** - vision-language model for medical image analysis

All documentation and code are published under the **AGPL v3 License** - ensuring that public resources remain public and preventing privatization of publicly-funded code.

### Security of the Open Source Approach

Open source for government systems is not a security risk - **it is a security advantage**. This principle has been known in cryptography for 140+ years as [Kerckhoffs' Principle (1883)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kerckhoffs's_principle): a system must be secure even if its architecture is public. All modern security standards (HTTPS, AES, RSA) are publicly published - and that is precisely why they are secure, because thousands of independent experts review them.

Detailed technical rationale for this approach, including the choice of Go programming language and modular monolith architecture, is available in the attachment: **`docs/technical-rationale.md`**

### Automated detection of dangerous errors

One of the key capabilities of the proposed platform is **AI-based detection of logical inconsistencies** in medical and social documentation. The system would automatically identify situations such as:

- **Impossible medical instructions** - e.g., instructing a patient with diagnosed blindness (diabetic retinopathy in both eyes) to "read values and write them down"
- **Inconsistency between diagnosis and recommendation** - e.g., concluding that treatment was "in accordance with good practice" when the patient was discharged with blood glucose of 0.7 mmol/L and a statement that "care is not ensured"

Such cases were documented during our analysis and represent a systemic risk to public health. An AI system can detect these inconsistencies in real-time, before they lead to harmful consequences.

### Proven effectiveness of physician + AI collaboration

Research demonstrates that the combination of physicians and AI systems significantly outperforms either alone:

- **66% of US physicians used AI in 2024** - a 78% increase from 2023 ([American Medical Association](https://www.ama-assn.org/practice-management/digital-health/2-3-physicians-are-using-health-ai-78-2023))
- **AI in radiology achieves 94% sensitivity** in detecting lung nodules, compared to 65-88% for radiologists ([Nature Communications Medicine](https://www.nature.com/articles/s43856-023-00388-5))
- **90% sensitivity in breast cancer detection** compared to 78% for radiologists ([Lancet Digital Health](https://www.thelancet.com/journals/landig/article/PIIS2589-7500(19)30235-2/fulltext))
- **FDA has approved ~950 AI medical devices** as of August 2024 ([NCBI](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK613808/))
- **AI reduces documentation time by up to 70%** - physicians focus on patients, not paperwork ([Mayo Clinic Proceedings: Digital Health](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11975979/))

The key is **collaboration**: AI has access to the complete medical history and can identify patterns that humans cannot, while the physician makes final decisions using clinical experience and context. Neither AI alone nor physician alone - together they are most effective.
